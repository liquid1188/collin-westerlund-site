The Record House

The label Collin founded in a house that was half recording studio — songwriting first, built for the little guy, and still his.

The Mission

For The Little Guy

Arkansas House Records set out to release original music that referenced the oldies and classics in their heyday — from Motown, Gamble & Huff, and R&B to rock n' roll and jazz — and to give artists the resources to chase a gold record.

The mission was simple: help talented singers and players who didn't have a band, didn't have songs, and didn't have anything released — by writing them the best songs possible. The label ran regular low-pressure meet-and-greet sessions where local musicians could plug in, play, and make mistakes, backed by a de facto house band. No big money. Local, and for the little guy.

The label has come home with Collin to Baltimore. The catalog, the songwriting-first mission, and the open-door house sessions are the blueprint — ready for the next room with a piano in it.

The Catalog

Label Releases

Kenny Goodman — Debut Album

Album · feat. "You Stay On My Mind," "All Ya' Need," "Move," "Look What Love Can Do," "Love & Heartbreak"

The label's flagship artist project: a full debut album produced at the house, with a run of music videos and AHR Live session recordings to match.

Ian Curriden — "Fire In My Heart"

Debut single · followed by The Sounds of Ian Curriden (2024) · North/South (East/West)

Curriden played saxophone on most of the label's releases before stepping to the mic: "Fire In My Heart," his first vocal collaboration with Collin, is an upbeat soul-funk song featuring both his raspy vocals and his horn. A Georgia-born, Northwest Arkansas–raised player influenced by Coltrane, Kenny Garrett, and the Charlie Parker records that first hooked him.

Avery Bruce — "When You Tell Me"

Single · December 6, 2023

The first collaboration between Collin and indie singer-songwriter Avery Rytel Bruce: a feisty, high-energy power-pop anthem. An Arkansas native with a theatre background, Avery writes introspective, genre-defying songs about love, loss, resilience, and self-discovery.

Press

In The News

In April 2023, the Arkansas Democrat-Gazette profiled the label: "Arkansas House Records has big plans for Bentonville music scene," by Monica Hooper. The piece covered the label's founding, its songwriting-first philosophy, the meet-and-greet sessions, the house band, and Collin's aim of building careers for artists who didn't yet have a catalog to their name.
